Total: $23.22bn in 2025 — 90% surge, best since 2014. Standing ovation. Also: when the guests leave, what did we build?

The 90% surge is real — Cardoso's monetary credibility deserves credit. But 96% is yield-chasing, not nation-building. Ethiopia attracted $3.4bn in FDI in the same period. Nigeria, with 10x the population, attracted $923m. The pipeline discount, the security premium, the regulatory friction — these are why factories keep getting built somewhere else.

 🧂  Portfolio money leaves faster than it arrived. CBN reserves fell six consecutive sessions this week — quietly, without drama — while the parallel spread widened from ₦27 to ₦31. Whispers in FX markets deserve to be heard early.
 💡 YOUR MONEY: Know what category your investments sit in. FPI-correlated assets move with global risk appetite. When the war ends and yields normalise, that $19.74bn does not call to say goodbye. FDI-correlated assets stay. Know which you own.

⚖️  VILLAIN & HERO
 
VILLAIN · DOWNGRADE

The Senate. Twelve weeks. At ₦700bn delayed economic activity per week, that is ₦8.4 trillion in roads, hospitals, and schools not built since January 1st. Nigeria is staring at a ₦30 trillion oil windfall. The legal machinery to receive it properly went home on Thursday. March 31 is Tuesday.

 
HERO · UPGRADE

CBN Governor Yemi Cardoso. Said publicly — without hedging — that the Middle East conflict is a major risk to Nigeria's economy. Central bankers don't usually do that. Then his FX architecture made our ₦1,545 prediction look professionally embarrassing. The EFEMS system held at $108 Brent. Give the man his flowers.
