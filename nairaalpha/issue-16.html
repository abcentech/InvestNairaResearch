THE 26.5% HOLD: CARDOSO LOCK SHOP ON CHEAP CREDIT
This na the big gist of mid-May 2026. For months, people bin dey hope say Oga Macro go drop interest rates, say bank go dash out cheap money to ginger businesses. Then April inflation show face at a stubborn 15.69%, and Brent crude just dey chest at $122.45/bbl. Cardoso look the matter, shake im head, and realize say to cut rate now na suicide. He chose a 'cautionary hold'. By keeping the MPR at 26.5%, the CBN don tell everyone say protecting the Naira from politicians' campaign cash is way more important than dashing companies expansion loans.
The hit is direct. If you are a business owner running on bank loans, that relief you dey wait for don fly out the window. Bros at the Pump no just dey pay for fuel at ₦1,320; he is now paying more for the POS machines and logistics to move goods. Asymmetric economics at its finest: the CBN hold rate for Abuja, but your bank manager still dey bill you high-tension lending rates for Lagos.
