The state absorbs the formation cost. The UK, Canada, or UAE absorbs the productive years. The $20 Billion in annual remittances that Nigeria celebrates as a macro story? That is Nigeria collecting a partial refund on an investment it already made. The rest of the dividend? It compounds in Toronto. And they send a Transferwise receipt at Christmas and call it patriotism.

The continent is running the same playbook in 14 different countries: educate the youth, charge them 25–35% to deploy that education, wonder why they leave, then celebrate the remittances as a development success. 🎓 THE ALPHA CLASSROOM — PAYMENT DOMICILIATION

Payment Domiciliation is a tripartite arrangement where a corporate buyer (like Shell) legally binds itself to pay the contractor’s invoice directly into a designated bank account managed by the contractor’s bank. This shifts the credit risk from the small subcontractor to the multi-billion-dollar buyer.

1. The Contract
Shell awards job to Contractor. 		→ 2. Domiciliation
Shell agrees to pay Bank directly. 		→ 3. The Cash
Bank disburses ₦ to Contractor.

Why this matters: If your business doesn’t have a Shell to guarantee your account, the bank treats you as B-minus risk and quotes 32% interest. Domiciliation is the financial elevator the street is denied.

At peak, a single 12.5kg refill cost ₦31,250 — 45% of the minimum wage — and millions of urban families switched back to firewood and charcoal. Not in 1985. In June 2026. Nigeria spent a decade encouraging the urban poor to upgrade to clean cooking. The price spike erased that in one quarter. The families who switched back will not easily switch forward again: the stoves are gone, the habits are reset, and the medical cost of indoor charcoal smoke will not appear in any CPI basket.
