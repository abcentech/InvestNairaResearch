EDITOR'S NOTE

Tinubu ended the wait this week. King Charles called Nigeria "an economic powerhouse" and dropped — we are not making this up — "Naija no dey carry last" at the state banquet. The whole country rejoiced.

Meanwhile, a market woman in Onitsha was explaining to her customer why the same basket of tomatoes now costs 36% more than it did in January. She did not use the phrase "economic powerhouse." She used other words.

 

King Charles declared Nigeria "an economic powerhouse" while February's food inflation landed like a quiet explosion: food prices jumped from 8.89% to 12.12% in a single month.

Nigeria's oil production fell 10% in February — meaning the windfall everyone is counting on arrived in a smaller envelope, with FAAC distributing ₦395 billion less than the previous month despite Brent above $90.

The 2026 budget still has not been signed. Eleven days left until March 31.

🏛️  THE MAIN STORY

Windsor, Onitsha, and the arrival that depends on where you're standing.

The last Nigerian president at Windsor was General Babangida in 1989 — before the internet, before mobile phones, before Afrobeats had a Wikipedia page. King Charles announced a "partnership of equals," called Nigeria a cultural force, mentioned Nollywood and Jollof rice in the same formal speech. UK-Nigeria trade hit a record £8.1 billion in 2025. Investor perception is a real asset, and this week moved it.

But the food inflation data released Monday — the same day Tinubu boarded his flight to London — tells a different arrival story. Beans, cassava, millet flour, yam flour, crayfish, ogbono — all went up. Every transport economist pointed at petrol, which added ₦300+ per litre since late February, inflating every link in the food supply chain.

The cruelest irony of this week: the oil price funding the diplomatic prestige at Windsor is the same oil price making it harder to boil yam in Ibadan. Oga Macro is managing both realities simultaneously. One photographs better.

 

"King Charles said Naija no dey carry last at Windsor Castle. He was correct. He was also speaking from a castle. The woman selling tomatoes in Onitsha was also correct. She was speaking from experience."

🛢️  THE WINDFALL THAT LEAKED

Why FAAC got smaller when oil got more expensive.

Nigeria's oil production fell 10% in February to 1.314 million barrels per day because the Bonga deepwater field went offline for maintenance. Bonga is Nigeria's largest single producing field. When it sneezes, FAAC catches a cold.

FAAC distributed ₦1.894 trillion in February — down ₦395 billion month-on-month. States budgeted for a windfall and got a shortfall. The ₦30 trillion NESG scenario requires both high prices and high production. February delivered one out of two. The windfall has a leak. The leak is called Bonga.

⚖️  THE VERDICT
 
VILLAIN OF THE WEEK  ·  DOWNGRADE

The NBS inflation framing. Not the data — the data is honest. But releasing a "inflation eases" headline while food prices jump 36% month-on-month is a communication failure. When people don't trust the framing of official numbers, they stop trusting the numbers. That trust is expensive to rebuild.

 
HERO OF THE WEEK  ·  UPGRADE

King Charles III. We mean this seriously. "Naija no dey carry last" at a formal state banquet — with Igbo, Hausa, and Yoruba proverbs, a Jollof rice joke, and a speech naming Nigeria a "cultural force" — is the kind of soft power moment money cannot buy. When the British monarch sounds like he follows Nigerian Twitter, something moves. S&P raised Nigeria's outlook to "positive." FATF grey list: done. This week added another layer.
