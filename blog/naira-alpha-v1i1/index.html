🛢️  SPECIAL SECTOR SPOTLIGHT: OIL & GAS — THE WORLD IS ON FIRE. NIGERIA HAS A LIGHTER. AND SOME COMPLICATIONS.
6:14.
AM. BERGER BRIDGE, LAGOS.
A man in a 2011 Toyota Camry with an 'I Love God' bumper sticker is in a fuel queue that began somewhere around 2019. The pump price is now ₦975 per litre. He does the maths twice. He did not make an error.
EDITOR'S NOTE

1,000km north, in a glass building in Abuja, a budget director is sipping Lipton tea and watching a Brent crude chart going up like the career of a Nollywood actor with a hit Netflix series. Brent just crossed $91 a barrel. He quietly saves the chart. He'll need it for the presentation.

Both men are right. Both men are in Nigeria. Welcome to the most complicated oil story on earth — and welcome to Naira Alpha, your weekly financial brief for people who deserve to understand what's happening to their money.

The US and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury on Iran on Saturday February 28th — the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, 24 tankers a day dropped to 4, and Brent crude surged +26% in a single week — its biggest gain since Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022.

Nigeria's 2026 budget was benchmarked at $64.85/barrel; at $91 every barrel now generates roughly $26 above benchmark straight into the federation account — but Nigeria produced 1.46 million bpd versus a target of 2.06 million, a 29% shortfall.

Meanwhile Dangote raised its pump gantry price by ₦101, NNPC Retail hit ₦975/litre, the CBN cut its MPR 50bps to 26.50%, and Tinubu quietly resolved the decade-long OPL 245 deepwater dispute — the sleeper story of the week.

🏛️  OGA AT THE TOP

Politics, policy, and the people who decide your wallet's fate

THE OPL 245 DEAL: THE SLEEPER STORY OF THE WEEK

While every financial journalist was watching oil charts, President Tinubu resolved one of the longest-running oil corruption scandals in Nigerian history. The OPL 245 deepwater licence — the block that spent a decade in courts across four continents — is finally unlocked. Eni CEO Claudio Descalzi flew into Abuja. The block is split into four new deepwater assets. At $91 oil, the timing is electric.

"Every journalist watched the oil price. Tinubu watched the oil block. In this specific instance: Oga Macro played chess."

CBN: THE RATE-CUTTER IN A RISING-PRICE WORLD

The CBN cut its MPR by 50 basis points to 26.50%. Inflation has fallen 10 months in a row — from a 34.19% peak to 15.10% today. The tension: this cut happened the same week Brent crossed $91. If fuel hits ₦1,000/litre before the next MPC meeting, Governor Cardoso has some explaining to do.

🧂  The CBN is driving forward while watching the rearview mirror. The rear view looks great. The windshield is murkier. We'll know in three weeks which mirror matters.

🏭  SECTOR SPOTLIGHT: OIL & GAS

The full anatomy of the most important energy week in two years

THE OIL WAR: A WEEK THAT MOVED MARKETS

Saturday Feb 28: US & Israel launch Operation Epic Fury. Ayatollah Khamenei killed. Sunday Mar 1: Iran retaliates. Strait of Hormuz "closed." 24 tankers/day drops to 4. QatarEnergy halts LNG production. Monday Mar 2: Brent +9% in a single session — biggest daily move since COVID. Dangote raises pump prices at midnight. Friday Mar 6: Brent closes at $91.23. Weekly gain: +26%.

"A third of the world's seaborne crude exits through the Strait of Hormuz. When it sneezes, Asia catches a cold and Nigeria cashes a cheque — or a bill. This week both arrived on the same day."

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR NIGERIA

Nigeria's 2026 budget was benchmarked at $64.85/barrel. At $91, every barrel generates roughly $26 above benchmark — straight into FAAC. But production is 1.46m bpd vs a 2.06m bpd target — a 29% shortfall. And ₦8.36 trillion of oil revenue is pre-committed to crude-backed loans before a single naira enters the federation account. The windfall is real. It passes through a very narrow pipe.

🧂  Nigeria is an oil-exporting nation that suffers when oil prices rise. The structural problem: we don't refine enough of what we produce. Until we fix that, high oil prices are a mixed blessing. This week was the proof.

🫴  We are an oil-producing country where fuel is expensive, a gas-producing country where cooking gas cost too much, and an electricity-generating country where light no dey. This is policy. And policy can change. Slowly.

💡 YOUR MONEY: Energy sector stocks (Seplat, Total Nigeria, Conoil) may not have fully priced in $91 Brent yet. For business owners: if your generator fuel cost just jumped 12%, reprice before your competition does. Solar + inverter is no longer a luxury — it's a hedge.

🔮  THE ALPHA

Our take. Our analysis. Our neck on the line.

Nigeria is an oil-exporting nation that suffers when oil prices rise. Read that again. Slowly. We produce the commodity. We don't refine enough of it. The theoretical windfall of $91 oil is real: extra FAAC, deficit reduction, reserve accumulation. The practical experience is ₦975/litre petrol, ₦1,400/kg cooking gas, ₦1,200/litre diesel, and a grid at 2,898 MW.

Every country that has solved this — Norway, UAE, Saudi Arabia — did it the same way: domestic refining at scale, a sovereign wealth fund, and deliberate policy to insulate citizens from commodity volatility. Nigeria is building all three. Dangote Refinery is Act One. Act Three — a functioning sovereign wealth fund that isn't raided — remains aspirational.

NAIRA ALPHA VERDICT — MARCH 7, 2026
⚡ CAUTIOUSLY BULLISH — WITH ACTIVE RISK FLAGS

Nigeria's macro fundamentals are the strongest in 13 years on multiple metrics. OPL 245: generational win. NGX: Africa's top performer. Reserves: 13-year high. BUT — petrol at ₦975 is a live consumer crisis. Hormuz closure could spike to $100+ and reignite inflation. Production is 29% below budget target. The windfall is real — partial, compressed, and threatened by the same dynamics that created it.

🎯  WATCH: Brent staying above $90 + Nigeria lifting above 1.6m bpd = the macro thesis holds. Either breaks = recalibrate.
